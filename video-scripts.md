# SpaceOps Onboarding Video Scripts

## VIDEO 1: Getting Started (Admin)
**Duration:** 3–4 minutes
**Audience:** New admin who just signed up
**Tone:** Calm, confident, no rush

---

### INTRO (15s)

**[Screen: SpaceOps login page]**

> Welcome to SpaceOps. In the next few minutes, you'll set up your first building, invite your team, and have your operation ready to go. Let's get started.

---

### SCENE 1: The Dashboard (30s)

**[Screen: Admin dashboard — empty state]**

> This is your admin dashboard. It's the command centre for your entire operation. Right now it's empty — but by the end of this video, you'll have a building, a team, and your first activity scheduled.

> On the left is your sidebar. Everything you need is here — buildings, users, checklists, reports, payroll, and settings.

---

### SCENE 2: Create a Building (60s)

**[Screen: Click Buildings in sidebar → New Building]**

> Let's add your first building. Click "Buildings" in the sidebar, then "New Building."

**[Screen: Fill in building form]**

> Enter the building name — something your team will recognise. Add the street address.

> The address sets the GPS coordinates for this building. This is important — when your janitors clock in, SpaceOps checks their location against these coordinates to verify they're actually on-site.

**[Screen: Building created → floor/room setup]**

> Your building is created with a ground floor by default. You can add more floors and define rooms for each one. If you have a floor plan image, upload it — SpaceOps can use AI to detect rooms automatically.

> Each room can be assigned a type — office, restroom, lobby, stairwell — which determines which checklist applies during inspections.

---

### SCENE 3: Invite Your Team (45s)

**[Screen: Click Users in sidebar → Invite User]**

> Now let's bring in your team. Click "Users," then "Invite User."

**[Screen: Fill in invite form]**

> Enter their name, email, and select a role.

> **Supervisors** manage day-to-day operations — they create activities, run inspections, and monitor attendance.

> **Janitors** see their assigned tasks for today, clock in and out, and report issues.

> **Clients** get a read-only view of their building's performance — pass rates, SLA metrics, and open issues.

> Each person gets an email with login instructions. They'll only see what's relevant to their role.

---

### SCENE 4: Create a Checklist (30s)

**[Screen: Click Checklists in sidebar → view default checklists]**

> SpaceOps comes with default checklists for common room types. You can use these as-is or customise them.

> A checklist defines what gets inspected in each room — "floors mopped," "bins emptied," "surfaces wiped." When a supervisor inspects a room, they work through this checklist item by item.

> You can also require photos or notes for specific items.

---

### SCENE 5: Settings (20s)

**[Screen: Click Settings → show pass threshold slider]**

> One more thing to set up. In Settings, you'll find the pass threshold — this is the minimum score a room needs to pass inspection. Most companies set this between 80% and 90%.

> You can also upload your organisation logo here. It'll appear on your reports and PDF exports.

---

### OUTRO (15s)

**[Screen: Dashboard with building visible]**

> That's it. You've got a building, a team, and your quality standards configured. In the next video, we'll show you how to create your first cleaning activity and run an inspection.

---

## VIDEO 2: Scheduling Activities (Supervisor)
**Duration:** 2–3 minutes
**Audience:** Supervisors
**Tone:** Practical, step-by-step

---

### INTRO (10s)

**[Screen: Supervisor dashboard]**

> As a supervisor, your main job in SpaceOps is scheduling cleaning activities, assigning rooms to your team, and making sure the work gets done. Here's how.

---

### SCENE 1: Create an Activity (60s)

**[Screen: Click Activities → New Activity]**

> Click "Activities" in the sidebar, then create a new one.

**[Screen: Activity creation form]**

> Give it a name — "Nightly clean — Floors 3 to 8" or "Restroom deep clean." Pick the building and floor.

> Set the scheduled date and time window. This tells your team when the work should happen.

> Now assign rooms. You'll see all the rooms on this floor. Select the ones that need cleaning and assign a janitor to each one — or assign them in bulk.

**[Screen: Activity published]**

> When you publish the activity, your assigned janitors will see their rooms when they log in. They can't start until they've clocked in.

---

### SCENE 2: Activity Templates (30s)

**[Screen: Click Templates]**

> If you run the same activity regularly — say, a nightly clean every weekday — save it as a template.

> Templates let you set up recurring schedules: daily, weekly, or monthly. SpaceOps generates the activities automatically. You set it once and it runs on its own.

---

### SCENE 3: One-Off Tasks (30s)

**[Screen: Click One-off Tasks → New Task]**

> Sometimes you need to assign something outside the regular schedule — a spill cleanup, a supply restock, a client request.

> One-off tasks are quick: title, description, due date, assign to a janitor. You can attach a photo for reference. The janitor sees it in their task list alongside their regular work.

---

### OUTRO (10s)

> That's scheduling. Your team knows what to do, where to do it, and when. Next, we'll cover inspections — how to check the work and record quality scores.

---

## VIDEO 3: Running Inspections (Supervisor)
**Duration:** 2–3 minutes
**Audience:** Supervisors
**Tone:** Precise, quality-focused

---

### INTRO (10s)

**[Screen: Supervisor dashboard showing activities]**

> Once your team has completed their cleaning tasks, it's time to inspect. SpaceOps makes inspections fast, structured, and consistent.

---

### SCENE 1: Start an Inspection (45s)

**[Screen: Click Inspections → Create Inspection]**

> Go to "Inspections" and create a new one. Select the activity you want to inspect.

> You'll see a list of rooms. Each room has a checklist — the one assigned to that room type.

**[Screen: Open a room's checklist]**

> Tap into a room to start inspecting. You'll work through the checklist item by item. Mark each one as passed or failed. If something fails, add a note explaining why.

> Some items may require a photo — take one on the spot.

---

### SCENE 2: QR Scanning (30s)

**[Screen: QR scanner view]**

> If your building uses QR codes on room doors, you can scan them to jump straight to that room's inspection. This speeds things up and ensures you're inspecting the right room.

---

### SCENE 3: Pass/Fail Results (30s)

**[Screen: Inspection results summary]**

> When you finish a room, SpaceOps calculates the pass rate automatically — the percentage of checklist items that passed.

> If the room scores above your organisation's pass threshold, it's marked as passed. Below that, it's failed and flagged for follow-up.

> The overall activity pass rate updates in real time on the dashboard.

---

### SCENE 4: Reporting Issues (20s)

**[Screen: Create a deficiency from inspection]**

> If you find a real problem — a broken fixture, a safety hazard, a recurring failure — report it as a deficiency. Set the severity: low, medium, or high. SLA timers start automatically based on severity.

---

### OUTRO (10s)

> Inspections are the backbone of quality control. Every pass rate, every report, every client metric starts here. Do them consistently and the data will speak for itself.

## VIDEO 4: The Janitor Experience
**Duration:** 2 minutes
**Audience:** Janitors (keep it simple and visual)
**Tone:** Friendly, clear, no jargon

---

### INTRO (10s)

**[Screen: Janitor login → Today view]**

> Here's everything you need to know about using SpaceOps as a janitor. It's simple — clock in, do your tasks, report any problems.

---

### SCENE 1: Clocking In (30s)

**[Screen: Clock-in screen with building selector]**

> When you arrive at your building, open SpaceOps and clock in.

> Select your building, then tap "Clock In." SpaceOps checks your GPS to confirm you're at the right location. If you're within range, you're verified.

> You need to clock in before you can see your tasks for today.

---

### SCENE 2: Today's Tasks (30s)

**[Screen: Today view with room list]**

> After clocking in, you'll see your assigned rooms for today. Each room shows what needs to be done.

> Tap a room to start working. When you're done, mark it as complete. Move to the next one.

> If you've been assigned a one-off task — like a spill cleanup or a supply run — it shows up here too.

---

### SCENE 3: Reporting an Issue (20s)

**[Screen: Report issue form]**

> If you find a problem — a broken light, a clogged drain, anything that needs attention — tap "Report Issue."

> Describe the problem, set the severity, and take a photo. Your supervisor gets notified and can assign someone to fix it.

---

### SCENE 4: Clocking Out (15s)

**[Screen: Clock-out button]**

> When your shift is done, tap "Clock Out." Your hours are recorded automatically and feed into attendance reports and payroll. No paper timesheets needed.

---

### OUTRO (10s)

> That's it. Clock in, do your rooms, report problems, clock out. SpaceOps handles the rest.

## VIDEO 6: Payroll (Admin)
**Duration:** 2 minutes
**Audience:** Admins
**Tone:** Practical, clear

---

### INTRO (10s)

**[Screen: Admin sidebar → Payroll]**

> SpaceOps calculates payroll directly from your team's clock-in and clock-out records. No manual timesheets, no estimating hours.

---

### SCENE 1: Setting Pay Rates (40s)

**[Screen: Payroll page → Pay Rates tab]**

> Start on the "Pay Rates" tab. You'll see a list of all your janitors and supervisors.

> Each person has three settings: hourly rate, overtime threshold, and overtime multiplier.

**[Screen: Click edit on an employee → edit dialog]**

> Click the pencil icon to set their rate. Enter their hourly pay. Set the overtime threshold — typically 40 hours per week. And the overtime multiplier — 1.5 for time-and-a-half, 2.0 for double time.

> If you don't set rates for an employee, SpaceOps uses defaults: fifteen dollars per hour, 40-hour threshold, 1.5 times overtime.

---

### SCENE 2: Generating Payroll (40s)

**[Screen: Payroll Runs tab → Generate Payroll dialog]**

> Switch to the "Payroll Runs" tab and click "Generate Payroll."

> Pick your pay period — start date and end date. Add an optional note like "Bi-weekly payroll, April 1 to 15."

> Click "Generate." SpaceOps pulls every clock-in and clock-out record for that period, calculates regular hours, overtime hours, and gross pay for every employee.

**[Screen: Payroll run detail page]**

> You'll see a summary: total gross pay, total hours, overtime hours, and employee count. Below that, a line-by-line breakdown for each person — their rate, hours, regular pay, overtime pay, and gross pay.

---

### SCENE 3: Approve and Export (20s)

**[Screen: Approve button → Export CSV]**

> Review the numbers. If everything looks right, click "Approve" to lock the run. Then "Export CSV" to download the data for your payroll provider.

> Approved runs can't be deleted — they're your audit trail. Draft runs can be deleted and regenerated if you need to make corrections.

---

### OUTRO (10s)

> That's payroll — from clock-in to pay calculation in a few clicks. No spreadsheets, no disputes, just actual hours and actual rates.

## VIDEO 7: Attendance and GPS Verification (Admin/Supervisor)
**Duration:** 1.5–2 minutes
**Audience:** Admins and supervisors
**Tone:** Matter-of-fact

---

### INTRO (10s)

**[Screen: Attendance page]**

> SpaceOps tracks every clock-in and clock-out with GPS verification. Here's what that looks like for you as a manager.

---

### SCENE 1: How GPS Verification Works (30s)

**[Screen: Building settings showing geofence radius]**

> Each building has a geofence — a virtual boundary around it. The default is 150 metres, but you can adjust it in building settings.

> When a janitor clocks in, SpaceOps captures their GPS coordinates and checks if they're within the geofence. If they are, the record is marked as "verified." If not, it's flagged.

> This isn't continuous tracking. SpaceOps only checks location once, at the moment of clock-in.

---

### SCENE 2: Viewing Attendance (30s)

**[Screen: Supervisor attendance dashboard]**

> Supervisors see today's attendance for their assigned buildings. You can see who's clocked in, when they arrived, and whether they were GPS verified.

**[Screen: Admin attendance view with history]**

> Admins can view attendance history across all buildings. Filter by date to see patterns — who's consistently on time, who's missing shifts, who's working overtime.

---

### SCENE 3: How It Connects (20s)

> Attendance data flows into two places automatically. Reports — where it appears as "Staff Hours" with total hours, shift counts, and averages. And Payroll — where it's used to calculate regular and overtime pay.

> No manual entry needed at any step.

---

### OUTRO (10s)

> GPS-verified attendance means you know who was on-site, when, and for how long. That's the foundation for accurate payroll and honest client reporting.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### General Guidelines
- **Screen recordings** should use a demo account with realistic data (3+ buildings, 5+ employees, recent activities with mixed pass/fail results)
- **Cursor movement** should be slow and deliberate — give viewers time to follow
- **Voiceover** should be recorded separately and laid over the screen recording
- **Music** — optional, very low, instrumental only. Not distracting
- **Resolution** — record at 1920x1080 minimum
- **Captions** — always include. Many users watch without sound, especially on mobile

### Suggested Recording Order
1. Set up the demo account with rich data first
2. Record all admin videos (1, 5, 6, 7) in one session
3. Record supervisor videos (2, 3) in one session
4. Record the janitor video (4) on a phone or mobile emulator

### Recommended Tools
- **Screen recording:** Loom, OBS Studio, or QuickTime (Mac)
- **Video editing:** CapCut (free), DaVinci Resolve (free), or Descript (AI-assisted editing with auto-captions)
- **Voiceover:** Record in a quiet room with a decent microphone. Descript can clean up audio
- **Hosting:** YouTube (unlisted or public), Vimeo, or embedded in the SpaceOps website/help centre

### Video Thumbnails
Each video should have a branded thumbnail using the SpaceOps design system:
- Warm off-white background (#FAFAF7)
- Video title in Geist Medium
- One key word in Instrument Serif italic
- Small SpaceOps logo mark in corner
- No screenshots in thumbnails — keep them clean and typographic
