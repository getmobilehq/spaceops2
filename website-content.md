Yes. The free plan includes one building and five team members with no time limit. You can run real operations on it — it's not a trial.

Absolutely. Upgrade to Pro or Enterprise whenever you need more buildings, more seats, or AI features. Downgrade at any time from your billing dashboard.

We accept all major credit and debit cards through Stripe. Enterprise customers can request invoice billing.

**Do you offer discounts for annual billing?**
Yes. Annual plans save 17% compared to monthly billing.

**Is my data secure?**
SpaceOps is built on Supabase with row-level security. Every user only sees data belonging to their organisation and role. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest.

**Can I import my existing buildings and rooms?**
You can set up buildings manually or upload floor plan images and let AI detect rooms automatically. For Enterprise customers, we offer assisted onboarding and data migration.

You'll see a prompt to upgrade. Nothing breaks — you just can't add more buildings or users until you upgrade or remove existing ones.

**Do janitors need their own account?**
Yes. Each janitor gets their own login so attendance, task completion, and issue reports are tracked individually. This is essential for accountability and payroll.

**Can my clients see the data?**
Yes. You can invite clients with a read-only client role. They see their buildings' pass rates, activity history, and open issues — but nothing about your internal operations, payroll, or other clients.

**Is there an API?**
Yes, on the Enterprise plan. The REST API gives you programmatic access to buildings, users, activities, and reports. Full OpenAPI documentation is included.

---

### Security Practices

**Row-Level Security**
Every database query is scoped to the authenticated user's organisation and role. Admins cannot see other organisations' data. Janitors cannot see other janitors' data. Clients only see their own buildings.

**Authentication & Access Control**
SpaceOps uses industry-standard JWT authentication with role-based access control. Four distinct roles (admin, supervisor, janitor, client) enforce the principle of least privilege across every screen and API endpoint.

**Encryption**
All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest. Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text.

**Infrastructure**
SpaceOps runs on enterprise-grade cloud infrastructure with automatic backups, redundancy, and uptime monitoring. Our database layer is powered by Supabase (built on PostgreSQL), one of the most trusted open-source database platforms.

**Audit Trails**
Platform-level audit logging tracks every significant action — who did what, when, and what changed. Payroll runs include creator and approver attribution with timestamps.

**API Security**
API access (Enterprise plan) is authenticated via API keys with per-key rate limiting and usage tracking. Keys can be revoked instantly from the admin dashboard.

### SECTION A: FAIR LABOR STANDARDS ACT (FLSA)

**Headline:** FLSA-Ready Timekeeping and Overtime

**Body:**
The Fair Labor Standards Act requires employers to keep accurate records of hours worked and to pay non-exempt employees overtime (1.5x) for any hours exceeding 40 in a workweek.

SpaceOps helps you meet these obligations:

**Accurate Timekeeping (29 CFR 516)**
The Department of Labor requires employers to maintain records of hours worked each day and total hours worked each workweek for every non-exempt employee. SpaceOps captures clock-in and clock-out timestamps automatically — no manual timesheets, no rounding disputes, no missing records.

**Overtime Calculation (29 CFR 778)**
SpaceOps calculates overtime automatically based on the threshold you configure per employee (default: 40 hours/week). The system separates regular hours from overtime hours and applies the correct multiplier (default: 1.5x, configurable up to 5x). This reduces the risk of miscalculating overtime pay — one of the most common FLSA violations.

**Recordkeeping Retention**
FLSA requires payroll records to be retained for at least 3 years, and timekeeping records for at least 2 years. SpaceOps stores all attendance and payroll data digitally with full timestamps, making retrieval straightforward during audits or disputes.

**Key Compliance Features:**
- Automated clock-in/clock-out with timestamps

- Prevents off-the-clock work by making attendance the gateway to task visibility

**Note:** SpaceOps provides timekeeping and calculation tools. It is not a payroll processor and does not file taxes, withhold deductions, or issue paycheques. Always consult your payroll provider and legal counsel for full FLSA compliance.

### SECTION C: GPS TRACKING AND EMPLOYEE PRIVACY

**Headline:** GPS Verification Done Right — With Transparency

**Body:**
SpaceOps uses GPS location at the moment of clock-in to verify that an employee is within the building's geofence radius. This is a single location check, not continuous tracking.

**What SpaceOps Captures:**
- GPS coordinates at the time of clock-in only
- Distance from the building's registered location
- Whether the employee was within the geofence radius (verified/unverified)

**What SpaceOps Does NOT Do:**
- No continuous or real-time location tracking throughout the shift
- No tracking of employees outside of the clock-in event
- No tracking of personal devices when not clocking in
- No background location collection

**State-Specific GPS/Privacy Considerations:**

**California (CCPA / CPRA)**
Under the California Privacy Rights Act, employee location data is considered personal information. Employers should provide notice in their privacy policy that GPS data is collected at clock-in for attendance verification. SpaceOps stores this data scoped to the organisation, and it can be exported or deleted upon request.

**Illinois (Workplace Privacy Act, 820 ILCS 55)**
Illinois restricts employers from tracking employees via GPS without consent. SpaceOps's geofence check occurs only during the voluntary clock-in action, and employers should ensure their employee handbook discloses this practice.

**Connecticut, Delaware, Texas, and Others**
Several states require employers to notify employees before using electronic monitoring. A clear attendance policy and employee acknowledgement form are recommended.

**Recommended Practices for SpaceOps Customers:**
1. Include GPS clock-in verification in your employee handbook
2. Have employees acknowledge the practice during onboarding
3. Explain what is captured (one-time location at clock-in) and what is not (continuous tracking)
4. Update your company privacy policy to reference location data collection
5. Provide a process for employees to raise concerns about location data

**Template Disclosure Language (for employee handbooks):**
"[Company Name] uses SpaceOps to manage attendance. When you clock in, the system captures your GPS location to verify that you are at the assigned work location. This is a one-time check at clock-in — your location is not tracked during or after your shift. This data is stored securely and used only for attendance verification and payroll purposes."

### SECTION H: JOINT EMPLOYER AND CONTRACTOR CONSIDERATIONS

**Headline:** Understand Joint Employer Risks in Facility Management

**Body:**
In the cleaning industry, joint employer liability is a significant concern. When a property management company or building owner exerts control over a cleaning contractor's workers — schedules, supervision, pay — they may be deemed a joint employer under the FLSA and NLRA.

**How SpaceOps Helps Clarify Boundaries:**
- The **admin** role belongs to the cleaning company, not the building owner
- The **client** role is read-only — clients cannot create tasks, assign workers, or manage schedules
- Scheduling, supervision, and payroll are controlled entirely by the cleaning company
- The client portal provides transparency without operational control

This role separation supports the argument that the building owner is a client receiving a service, not a joint employer directing work. However, joint employer determinations are fact-specific — consult legal counsel for your situation.
